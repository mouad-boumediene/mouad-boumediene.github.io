Research

My recent research includes developing HM-DRL—a Deep Reinforcement Learning approach for distributed multi-robot pathfinding—and exploring relocation strategies to reduce bottlenecks in large robot fleets. I’ve also worked on 4D trajectory planning for multiple UAVs.

Career

  • 2023 – 2024: Postdoctoral Researcher, MMMI - University of Southern Denmark, Odense, Denmark
    - Contributed to the “Swarm Robotics for Industry 4.0” project, under grant 0136-00251B
    - Developed HM-DRL, a Deep Reinforcement Learning approach for distributed MAPF (now under final revision)
    - Developed Relocation-MAPF to enhance efficiency and success rate for robot fleets
  • Feb 2023: Robotics Researcher (PhD Internship), Syddansk Universitet - University of Southern Denmark
    - Studied DRL for swarms of AMRs in Industry 4.0 environments
    - Contributed to the “Swarm Robotics for Industry 4.0” project (grant 0136-00251B)
  • 2021 – 2022: Robotics Engineer (Freelance), AiGro (Netherlands, remote)
    - Built a 2D simulator for pathfinding/localization algorithms in agricultural robots
    - Implemented a sensor fusion approach using a Kalman filter for real data (IMU + GPS)
  • 2019 – 2022: Temporary Lecturer, University of 20 août 1955, Skikda, Algeria
    - Taught advanced signal processing and digital regulation (labs + tutorials)
    - Supervised Master’s students’ final-year projects
