About Me

Join me, a dedicated postdoctoral researcher at SDU, in exploring the future of robotics by developing innovative methods to coordinate teams of robots in shared environments. My interests include decentralized multi-robot planning, advanced concurrency, and large-scale simulation experiments.

Beyond my core projects, I actively publish robotics coding tutorials on my YouTube channel, firmly believing in the power of knowledge sharing through small-scale projects. It's my way of giving back to the tech community and gathering broader feedback on my work.

Projects

DRL-Based Pathfinding for Multi-Robot Systems

This project implements a decentralized approach to multi-robot path planning using deep reinforcement learning. It focuses on dynamic obstacle avoidance and cooperative behavior within cluttered environments.

Scalability Testing & Large-Scale Simulations

A series of experiments to measure how effectively multi-robot algorithms scale with an increasing number of agents. Focus on real-time performance monitoring and concurrency.

Multi-UAV 4D Trajectory Planning

Each agent reserves the airspace using 4D boxes for a safer flight. It employs a stochastic-based discretization approach to handle real-time changes in the environment.
